## Build Process (Ordered Steps)

1. **Audit current GBP state** — Pull or review the client's current GBP data. Score completeness percentage. Identify every empty or suboptimal field. Reference local_audit findings if available.

2. **Optimize primary category** — Research all available Google categories for the client's business type. Select the most specific category that matches the core offering. Document why this category was chosen and what alternatives were considered. This is the highest-impact single change.

3. **Select secondary categories** — Add 2-5 secondary categories that capture additional services the business offers. Secondary categories influence which searches trigger the listing but carry less weight than primary.

4. **Write business description** — 750-character max. Include the primary service, service area, unique value proposition, and 2-3 natural keyword mentions. No promotional language, no URLs, no ALL CAPS — Google guidelines prohibit these.

5. **Complete all attributes** — Fill every applicable attribute: accessibility features, amenities, crowd demographics, health/safety measures, payment methods, planning considerations. More complete attributes = stronger completeness signal.

6. **Optimize photos** — Ensure minimum 10 photos covering: exterior (at least 3), interior, team/staff, products, services in action. All photos must be geo-tagged (EXIF data with business location coordinates) and have descriptive filenames. Remove any stock photos.

7. **Build service menu** — Create a comprehensive service list with individual descriptions (150-300 characters each). Include pricing where the business is comfortable. Group services into logical categories.

8. **Set up Google Posts strategy** — Create a 4-post monthly calendar: 1 offer/promotion, 1 event or update, 1 product/service spotlight, 1 educational/tips post. Each post includes an image, 100-300 words, and a CTA button.

9. **Seed Q&A section** — Pre-populate with 10-15 commonly asked questions and answers. Monitor for new questions weekly and respond within 24 hours.

10. **Verify hours and special hours** — Confirm regular hours are accurate. Pre-load holiday hours and special closures for the next 90 days. Set up a reminder system for seasonal hour changes.

11. **Configure SAB settings** — If applicable, set service areas correctly (cities/regions, not radius). Hide the physical address per Google guidelines. Ensure the website compensates with location pages for each service area.

## Critical Patterns

### 1. Primary Category Selection Is the Highest-Impact Single Action
**WHEN:** Setting or reviewing the GBP primary category.
**HOW:** Research all available Google categories (Google's category list changes quarterly). Select the most specific option: "Personal Injury Attorney" beats "Lawyer," "Emergency Plumber" beats "Plumber," "Vegan Restaurant" beats "Restaurant." Compare against top 3 competitors' primary categories.
**WHY:** Primary category is the single most influential local ranking factor per 2026 research. The wrong primary category means the listing doesn't appear for the most valuable queries.
**DON'T:** Choose a broad category "to cover more searches." Specificity outranks breadth in category selection.

### 2. Google Posts Are Weekly Activity Signals
**WHEN:** Managing ongoing GBP presence.
**HOW:** Publish minimum 4 posts per month (1/week). Post types rotate: offers, events/updates, product/service spotlights, educational content. Each post: relevant image (not stock), 100-300 words, CTA button (Learn More, Call Now, Book, etc.). Posts expire after 7 days unless they're events with end dates.
**WHY:** Google Posts signal business activity. Profiles without recent posts are perceived as less active. Posts also appear directly in the business listing, increasing engagement.
**DON'T:** Publish text-only posts or reuse the same image across all posts. Each post should be visually distinct and topically varied.

### 3. Photo Geo-Tagging Reinforces Location Signals
**WHEN:** Uploading or optimizing GBP photos.
**HOW:** Before uploading, verify EXIF data includes GPS coordinates matching the business address. Use descriptive filenames: "smith-law-office-conference-room-downtown-seattle.jpg" not "IMG_4567.jpg." Upload minimum 10 photos across categories. Add new photos monthly.
**WHY:** Businesses with 100+ photos get 520% more calls than those with fewer than 5. Geo-tagged photos provide an additional location signal that reinforces the business address.
**DON'T:** Use stock photos. Google can detect them and they provide no location signal. Real business photos are mandatory.

### 4. Q&A Section Is a Pre-Emptive Content Opportunity
**WHEN:** Setting up or maintaining the Q&A section.
**HOW:** Seed 10-15 questions that real customers commonly ask: hours, parking, services offered, pricing ranges, appointment requirements, insurance acceptance. Answer each thoroughly. Monitor weekly for new user-submitted questions and respond within 24 hours.
**WHY:** Anyone can ask and answer questions on a GBP listing. Unanswered questions or incorrect answers from random users hurt credibility. Pre-seeding controls the narrative.
**DON'T:** Leave the Q&A section empty and hope for the best. Proactive seeding is standard practice.

### 5. Service Menu Drives Service-Specific Queries
**WHEN:** Building the services section of GBP.
**HOW:** List every service the business provides as an individual item. Write a unique 150-300 character description for each. Include pricing if the business is comfortable. Group into logical categories. Use natural language that matches how customers search.
**WHY:** Google uses service menu items to match the listing to service-specific queries ("emergency AC repair near me"). Missing services = missing from those search results.
**DON'T:** List services with one-word entries and no descriptions. Each service needs enough context for Google to match it to relevant queries.

### 6. Hours Accuracy Prevents Trust Erosion
**WHEN:** Setting up or maintaining hours.
**HOW:** Verify regular hours are correct. Pre-load special hours for all known holidays and closures for the next 90 days. Set a quarterly reminder to update seasonal hours. If the business has different hours for different services, use the "More hours" feature.
**WHY:** A customer who arrives at a "closed" business that Google said was open leaves a 1-star review. Hours inaccuracy is one of the top reasons for negative reviews.
**DON'T:** Set hours once and forget. Hours need proactive management with a calendar of upcoming changes.

### 7. Attributes Complete the Listing's Detail Signal
**WHEN:** Filling out GBP attributes.
**HOW:** Go through every available attribute category: accessibility (wheelchair access, Braille signage), amenities (Wi-Fi, parking), crowd information, health/safety, payment methods, planning requirements (appointments, reservations). Fill every applicable attribute.
**WHY:** Each filled attribute adds to the completeness signal. Attributes also power filtered searches ("wheelchair accessible restaurants near me"). Missing attributes = missing from filtered results.
**DON'T:** Skip attributes because they seem minor. Completeness is cumulative — each attribute contributes to the overall signal strength.

### 8. SAB vs. Storefront — Different Optimization Paths
**WHEN:** The client is a service area business.
**HOW:** SABs must hide their physical address per Google guidelines when they don't serve customers at that location. Set service areas by city/region (not radius). Compensate for the ranking disadvantage of a hidden address by maximizing every other factor: reviews (aggressive solicitation), GBP completeness (100% target), service area pages on the website (one per major service city), local content (city-specific case studies).
**WHY:** There's a negative correlation between hiding address and local pack ranking. SABs must overcompensate on controllable factors.
**DON'T:** Show the address for an SAB that doesn't serve walk-in customers. Google will suspend the listing.

### 9. Business Description Must Follow Google Guidelines
**WHEN:** Writing or reviewing the GBP business description.
**HOW:** 750 characters max. Include: what the business does, who it serves, service area, and what makes it unique. Use 2-3 natural keyword mentions. Prohibited: URLs, phone numbers, pricing, promotional language ("best in town"), all caps, emojis.
**WHY:** Descriptions that violate guidelines get removed by Google. A well-written description helps both Google and potential customers understand the business.
**DON'T:** Stuff keywords or write marketing copy. Google's guidelines are clear — violation risks profile suspension.

### 10. Monthly GBP Insights Tracking
**WHEN:** Reporting on GBP performance.
**HOW:** Pull GBP Insights monthly: profile views (Search vs. Maps), customer actions (calls, website clicks, direction requests), photo views vs. competitor average, search queries triggering the listing. Track trends month-over-month.
**WHY:** GBP Insights show whether optimization changes are producing results. Without tracking, optimization is guesswork.
**DON'T:** Optimize GBP and never check Insights. Monthly tracking closes the feedback loop between changes and results.

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | What To Do Instead |
|---|---|---|
| Choosing a broad primary category to "cover more searches" | Specific categories rank for specific queries; broad categories lose to specific competitors | Research all subcategories; choose the most specific match |
| Setting up GBP once and never maintaining it | GBP signals require ongoing activity (posts, photos, Q&A responses) | Establish weekly/monthly management cadence |
| Uploading stock photos to GBP | Google detects stock photos; they provide no geo-signal; customers distrust them | Use only real, geo-tagged business photos |
| Writing keyword-stuffed business descriptions | Violates Google guidelines; risks profile suspension | Write naturally with 2-3 organic keyword mentions |
| Ignoring the Q&A section | Random users can post misleading answers that hurt the business | Pre-seed with accurate answers; monitor weekly |
| Publishing the same Google Post every week | Repetitive content signals low effort; reduces engagement | Rotate post types: offers, updates, spotlights, educational |
| Showing SAB address when no walk-in service exists | Direct violation of Google guidelines; risks suspension | Hide address; set service areas; compensate with other factors |
| Listing services without descriptions | Google can't match one-word services to specific search queries | Write 150-300 character descriptions for every service |
